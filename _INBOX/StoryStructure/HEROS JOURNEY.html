The Hero's Journey - Joseph Campbell

1. Departure (The Call to Adventure)

  • 1.1. The Ordinary World
    • Comfort Zone: Hero is in a familiar environment.
    • Character Flaws: Weaknesses or unresolved issues that the hero must address.
    • Status Quo: Society or surroundings are relatively stable.
  • 1.2. The Call to Adventure
    • Trigger Event: Something happens that disrupts the hero’s normal life.
    • External or Internal Call: The challenge could be external (a physical event) or internal (a personal need).
  • 1.3. Refusal of the Call
    • Fear of Change: Resistance to leaving the known.
    • Rationalization: Hero may offer excuses for not going.
    • Reluctance: Emotional or physical hesitation.
  • 1.4. Meeting with the Mentor
    • Mentor as Guide: Provides wisdom or gifts to the hero.
    • Supernatural Aid: Could involve magical items or knowledge.
    • Preparation: Helps the hero mentally or physically prepare.
  • 1.5. Crossing the First Threshold
    • Leaving Comfort: Hero leaves the Ordinary World for the unfamiliar.
    • First Test of Will: Initial challenges or risks are faced.
    • Point of No Return: There is no turning back after this threshold.
  • 1.6. Belly of the Whale
    • Abyss: The hero faces a deep emotional or psychological challenge.
    • Dismantling Ego: The hero’s identity begins to dissolve.
    • Rebirth: The first hints of transformation arise.

2. Initiation (The Road of Trials)

  • 2.1. The Road of Trials
    • Progressive Challenges: The trials increase in difficulty.
    • Growth Through Failure: Hero often fails and learns from mistakes.
    • Allies and Enemies: Friends are gained, and enemies are encountered.
  • 2.2. Meeting with the Goddess
    • Feminine Wisdom: May represent the hero’s integration of anima/animus.
    • Inspiration or Love: Represents emotional fulfillment or a deep spiritual revelation.
    • Creative Force: The goddess may symbolize fertility or the generation of new ideas.
  • 2.3. Woman as Temptress
    • Distractions: Material or emotional desires attempt to lead the hero astray.
    • Tests of Purity: Hero must resist indulgence or ego-driven actions.
    • Psychological Conflict: Represents inner struggles with desires and impulses.
  • 2.4. Atonement with the Father
    • Confrontation: Hero faces a powerful figure or force.
    • Inner Reconciliation: Represents a psychological or spiritual union with one’s higher self.
    • Facing Mortality: Can symbolize the hero's acceptance of death or ultimate truth.
  • 2.5. Apotheosis
    • Illumination: A breakthrough moment of insight.
    • Union of Opposites: Harmonizing opposing forces within or outside the hero.
    • Spiritual Enlightenment: Symbolizes the hero’s rise to a new level of consciousness.
  • 2.6. The Ultimate Boon
    • Gift of Knowledge or Power: The hero may acquire a weapon, wisdom, or some other key boon.
    • Transformation of the Self: Represents inner growth and accomplishment.
    • Completion of Quest: Hero fulfills the central objective of their journey.

3. Return (The Hero's Return to the Ordinary World)

  • 3.1. Refusal of the Return
    • Desire to Remain: Hero feels fulfilled in the new world.
    • Fear of Incomprehension: Hero fears being misunderstood or unable to integrate new knowledge into the old world.
  • 3.2. The Magic Flight
    • Escape or Pursuit: The hero is chased or must flee with the prize.
    • Returning Under Pressure: The journey home is urgent or perilous.
    • Desperation and Obstacles: The hero encounters more trials before returning.
  • 3.3. Rescue from Without
    • External Aid: Allies or mentors help the hero return.
    • Supernatural Help: Divine or magical intervention may assist.
    • Collapse or Exhaustion: Hero may collapse, physically or emotionally, requiring outside help.
  • 3.4. Crossing the Return Threshold
    • Re-integration: Hero returns to their former society.
    • Challenge of Sharing Knowledge: The hero must now reconcile new wisdom with old norms.
    • Return to Mundanity: Hero faces the prospect of returning to an ordinary life.
  • 3.5. Master of Two Worlds
    • Spiritual and Material Balance: The hero balances the inner and outer worlds.
    • Freedom to Live: Having overcome fear, the hero can now live fully in both worlds.
    • Harmony with Self: The hero embodies wisdom, integrating lessons from the journey.
  • 3.6. Freedom to Live
    • Life Without Fear: The hero is free from existential worries.
    • Contentment: There is an acceptance of the ebb and flow of life.
    • Return to Purpose: Hero may now embark on a new mission, or live in peace, having transcended former limitations.
